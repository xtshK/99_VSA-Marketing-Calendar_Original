## Step 1: Identify the Campaign Audience Type

Before anything else, ask:

> "Who is this campaign targeting?
> **A) End users** — buyers, IT admins, teachers, facilities managers (standard VMM campaign)
> **B) Channel partners** — resellers, distributors, VARs; marketing TO them to build ViewSonic product awareness, preference, and sell-through capability
> **C) Co-marketing with a partner** — marketing WITH a specific partner, targeting that partner's customers together to promote ViewSonic solutions"

This answer changes the personas, messaging angle, media mix, and what success looks like. Route B and C briefs use adapted section structures — see below.

---

## Step 2: Scope the Brief

Then ask:

> "What are we briefing today?
> **1)** Full campaign — multiple deliverables, multiple media (launch, seasonal push, demand gen)
> **2)** Single asset — one deliverable (a video, one email, a landing page, a print piece, an ad)
> **3)** Quick creative — a simple, fast one-off (social image, banner resize, copy check, translation)"

Also confirm if not already clear:
- **Vertical** — Education (K-12 / Higher Ed), Enterprise, Government, or Consumer?
- **Product or solution focus**
- *For Route C only:* Partner name, their customer profile, and what the partner is contributing

Do not proceed without knowing the audience route, tier, and vertical — all three change the brief significantly.

## TIER 1 — Full Campaign Brief

*Use when: multiple deliverables, multiple media, or a coordinated launch/push.*

### 1. Campaign Overview
- **Campaign name** — use the standard naming convention: `REGION_QUARTER_VERTICAL_PRODUCTNAME or CAMPAIGN NAME`
  - Region: `US` / `CA` / `LA` — confirm with the VMM before generating
  - Vertical: `BUS` / `EDU` / `GOV` / `CHN`
  - Product: full model (`IFP7551`), series (`IFP51 Series`), or descriptive name (`Choose Your Monitor`)
  - Example: `US_Q3_BUS_VG51 Choose Your Monitor`
- Vertical (Education / Enterprise / Government / Consumer)
- Product or solution focus
- Campaign goal (awareness, demand gen, launch, sales enablement, event)
- Timeline / key dates / hard launch date

### 2. Target Audience
- Primary persona(s) — pull from buyer personas context
- Secondary persona(s) or influencers
- Where they are in the funnel (awareness / consideration / decision)

### 3. Key Message
Ask the VMM these prompts before drafting:
- **What pain point is this solving for the end user?**
- **What problem are we trying to solve?**
- **What are we trying to get across?**

Then capture:
- One-sentence campaign message (plain language, 6th–9th grade)
- Supporting proof points (3 max)
- What we want them to think, feel, and do after seeing this campaign

### 4. Competitive Context *(Optional — skip if this campaign has no competitive angle)*
- Who we're up against for this initiative
- Our positioning angle — what we're leaning into vs. them
- Flag if differentiators are marked [PLACEHOLDER] and note that PM input is needed

### 5. Media, Deliverables and Sales Enablement
**Media**
- Recommended media (web, email, social, blog, paid, events, partner programs) — based on what's strategically right, not constrained by form type
- Rationale for each media selection
- Rough content cadence

**Campaign Visual Direction** *(campaign-level — sets the overall look and feel for all assets in this campaign)*
- **Overall visual tone:** e.g., clean and technical, warm and human, aspirational, bold and direct
- **Photography style:** Studio/product-isolated vs. environmental/in-context; high-key vs. natural lighting
- **Brand tier:** Premium flagship treatment vs. standard campaign vs. value/accessibility-focused
- **People direction (campaign-wide):** General guidance on whether people appear across assets — specific casting per asset is in the tactic blocks below
- **Color and lighting:** Any campaign-specific palette direction beyond standard ViewSonic brand guidelines
- **References or mood board:** Links or descriptions of visual references, previous campaigns, or samples that set the direction

> *Asset-specific image type, people, and on-screen image are captured per tactic in the blocks below — this section sets the campaign-wide visual language that all assets should stay consistent with.*

---

| # | Asset | Type | Go Live Date | Priority |

**Tactic Direction**

Below the table, add a direction block for each asset. If the VMM has not provided tactic direction, Claude should suggest angles based on the campaign context, goals, and key message already captured in the brief — flagged clearly as recommendations for VMM review. The VMM owns the final brief quality and must confirm all tactic directions before the brief goes to the creative team.

Build all tactic direction blocks and present everything to the VMM in one pass for review and edits.

**[Asset name from row #1]**
- **Angle / Hook:** What is the specific angle of attack? What is the one idea this asset leads with? *(VMM to confirm — Claude will suggest if not provided)*
- **Copy Direction:** What does this asset need to communicate? Key message, tone, and any specific points to hit
- **CTA:** What action should this drive? (e.g., "Learn More", "Watch the Video", "Request a Demo", "Download the Guide")
- **Visual Direction:**
  - Image type: Product-focused or Scenario-based
  - People: Yes (specify: who, doing what) / No
  - On-screen Image: What is displayed on the screen of the product? Be specific — e.g., "myViewBoard 3.0 interface with classroom lesson materials", "Excel report open in a corporate desktop scenario", "product spec overlay on a dark background". *(VMM to confirm — Claude will suggest based on product and campaign context if not provided)*

*(Repeat the direction block for each asset in the table)*

> **VMM sign-off note:** Before sending this brief to the creative team, review all Angle / Hook and Copy Direction fields above. These are the most critical inputs for the creative team — vague or missing direction is the #1 cause of rework. If Claude suggested an angle, confirm it reflects your intent or rewrite it.

**Sales Enablement Assets**
- List any sales-specific assets needed as part of this campaign (e.g., battlecard, one-pager, competitive leave-behind)
- Target go live date for each asset and priority order

### 6. Success Metrics
- What does success look like? (leads, MQLs, web traffic, email open rate, etc.)
- Baseline if known

## TIER 2 — Single Asset Brief

*Use when: one deliverable is being requested.*

### 1. Asset Overview
- Asset type (video, email, landing page, print piece, ad, social image, etc.)
- Vertical and target audience
- Campaign or initiative this supports (or "standalone")
- Target go live date
- Is this a hard deadline?

### 2. Target Audience
- Who is the primary reader/viewer? (specific persona — teacher, IT admin, procurement officer, etc.)
- Where are they in the funnel?

### 3. Key Message
Ask before drafting:
- **What pain point is this trying to solve?**

Then capture:
- One-sentence message this asset needs to communicate
- 2–3 supporting points
- Primary call to action

### 4. Visual Direction
- **Image/video type:** Product-focused or Scenario-based
- **People:** Yes (specify: who, doing what) / No
- **On-screen Image:** What is displayed on the screen of the product? Be specific — e.g., "myViewBoard 3.0 with active lesson", "PowerPoint open on a productivity monitor", "blank/hero shot with no screen content". *(VMM to confirm — Claude will suggest based on product and campaign context if not provided)*
- **Tone:** e.g., aspirational, educational, product-focused, lifestyle
- **References or samples:** Any visual direction or existing assets to align with

### 5. Asset-Specific Requirements

**If video:**
- Duration (e.g., :15, :30, 60-sec, YT Short)
- Format (landscape / vertical / square)
- Voiceover: Yes / No
- On-screen talent: Yes / No — if yes, describe
- Script needed: Yes / No
- Music/SFX direction

**If email:**
- Email type (HTML designed / text-based)
- Subject line direction
- Preheader direction
- Number of CTAs (recommend: 1 primary)
- Personalization needed?

**If landing page:**
- Primary conversion goal (form fill, demo request, download, purchase)
- Key sections needed (hero, features, testimonial, CTA, FAQ)
- Form fields required
- SEO/GEO target query

**If print/PDF (sell sheet, brochure, flyer, battle card, etc.):**
- Dimensions / format
- Will it be printed? (affects bleed, color mode)
- Key sections / content blocks needed

**If digital ad (web ad, social ad, banner):**
- Ad sizes / dimensions needed
- Static or animated
- Platform (LinkedIn, Google Display, programmatic, etc.)

**If social image:**
- Platform (LinkedIn, Instagram, Facebook, X)
- Image ratio / dimensions
- Text overlay: Yes / No

### 6. Brief Sign-Off
Complete the sign-off block at the end of this brief before submitting to the creative team.

## ROUTE B — Channel Partner-Facing Brief Adaptations

*Apply these adjustments when audience type is Route B (marketing TO channel partners). Use the same tier structure (Tier 1, 2, or 3) but replace end-user-focused sections with the partner-facing versions below. Reference `context/07-channel-marketing.md` for the full channel landscape, ADF funding process, and parallel rail timing rules.*

### Campaign Overview (Route B)
- **Campaign name** — use the standard naming convention: `REGION_QUARTER_CHN_PRODUCTNAME or CAMPAIGN NAME`
  - Route B always uses `CHN` as the vertical code
  - Example: `US_Q3_CHN_IFP51 Series Partner Launch`
- Partner type focus (resellers / distributors / VARs / all)
- Product or solution focus
- Campaign goal (partner awareness, sell-through enablement, training, deal registration, MDF-funded activity)
- Timeline / key dates

### Target Audience (Route B)
- Primary partner persona (e.g., reseller sales rep, distributor product manager, VAR solution architect)
- What motivates them to push one brand over another (margins, support, ease of demo, win rate)
- Where they are in partner engagement (new partner, active seller, dormant)

### Key Message (Route B)
Ask before drafting:
- **What do partners currently think about ViewSonic — and what do we want them to think instead?**
- **What makes ViewSonic easier or more profitable to sell than the competition?**
- **What's the one thing a partner sales rep should say to their customer about ViewSonic?**

Then capture:
- One-sentence partner-facing message
- Supporting proof points relevant to partners (margins, deal registration, support responsiveness, training availability)
- Desired partner action (register a deal, attend a webinar, demo the product, push ViewSonic over a competitor)

### Media, Deliverables and Sales Enablement (Route B)
**Media:** Partner portal, partner email programs, distributor newsletters, trade events (InfoComm, AVIXA), partner webinars, channel sales team enablement materials

**Asset List:** Partner-specific assets — co-op funded materials, partner sell sheets, competitive battlecards for partner use, demo guides, training decks, partner incentive communications
- Include target go live date for each asset

**Visual Direction:** Apply same product-focused vs. scenario-based and people rules, but scenarios should show partner sales contexts (demo room, customer presentation) not end-user contexts. On-screen Image should reflect a sales or demo scenario — e.g., "ViewSonic product page on screen", "demo interface running in a showroom setting".

### Success Metrics (Route B)
- Partner engagement rate, deal registration volume, sell-through rate
- Partner webinar attendance, training completion
- Competitive win rate in partner-assisted deals

## ROUTE C — Co-Marketing Brief Adaptations

*Apply these adjustments when audience type is Route C (marketing WITH a partner, targeting their customers). Use the same tier structure but include the partner profile block and adjust audience, messaging, and channels. Reference `context/07-channel-marketing.md` for partner program context and hand-off timing rules.*

### Partner Profile *(Route C only — add before all other sections)*
- Partner name and type
- Partner's customer profile (who are their customers — IT directors at mid-market companies, K-12 district buyers, etc.)
- What the partner is contributing (their customer list, co-op/MDF funds, event presence, social amplification, etc.)
- What ViewSonic is contributing (creative, product, brand, co-op funds)
- Brand guidelines for co-branded materials (how both logos appear, who leads messaging)

### Target Audience (Route C)
- The partner's end customers — describe their role, vertical, and buying priorities
- Note where they overlap with or differ from standard ViewSonic buyer personas

### Key Message (Route C)
Ask before drafting:
- **What does this partner's customer care most about that ViewSonic can speak to?**
- **What does the customer get from this partnership that they couldn't get from either brand alone?**
- **What pain point are we solving together?**

Then capture:
- One-sentence joint campaign message
- Supporting proof points from both ViewSonic and the partner
- Desired customer action

### Media, Deliverables and Sales Enablement (Route C)
**Media:** Partner's owned media (their email list, their events, their social) plus ViewSonic-amplified media; note which partner is driving which

**Asset List:** Co-branded assets — note which assets are partner-led vs. ViewSonic-led; both brands must appear on all shared materials
- Include target go live date for each asset

**Visual Direction:** Same product-focused vs. scenario-based and people rules apply; ensure brand balance between ViewSonic and partner visuals. On-screen Image should reflect the joint use case — confirm with the VMM what content fits both brands' context.

### Success Metrics (Route C)
- Joint leads generated, co-branded campaign reach
- Partner-attributed pipeline and revenue
- Partner satisfaction with the co-marketing program

## Style Rules (All Tiers)
Apply all ViewSonic brand voice and style rules throughout:
- Confident, Innovative, Personal, Approachable
- No em dashes, no Oxford commas
- Conversational, contractions OK
- 6th–9th grade reading level

## If Information Is Missing
Ask the user for the vertical, product focus, and campaign goal before drafting. Do not guess the vertical — it changes tone, personas, and competitors entirely.

If competitive differentiators are needed and the placeholder is not filled in, flag it explicitly: "Note: Competitive differentiators for [product] are pending PM input. I've noted this in the brief — fill it in before sharing externally."
